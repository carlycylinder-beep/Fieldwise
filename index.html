How We Work

Our approach draws on Lean Six Sigma and Industrial-Organizational Psychology, the same principles used by leading companies, adapted for growing trade and service businesses.

Fieldwise identifies operational opportunities, implements practical improvements, strengthens organizational systems, and creates lasting change. This isn't coaching, a consulting report, or a theoretical strategy session. It's direct, hands-on implementation.

1

Observe

Real conversations with your team, and real time watching how work actually happens across departments and locations, not just how it's described on paper. That means meeting people where they are, not coming in strong, and genuinely listening before making changes.

2

Measure

Data is pulled from existing reports, workflows, and day-to-day operations to understand where time, money, and consistency are being lost, and the actual workflow is observed in action to spot the real bottlenecks. Decisions are based on evidence, not assumptions.

3

Diagnose the Root Cause

Symptoms and root causes are rarely the same thing. The underlying process gets fixed, not just the visible issue, so the same problem doesn't quietly resurface somewhere else in the organization a few weeks later.

4

Implement

Clear systems, documentation, and accountability structures get built directly into how the organization operates, whether leadership is in the room or not. Real protocols and workflows, not just ideas on a whiteboard.

5

Sustain the Change

Systems only work if people actually use them. That means understanding how teams operate, building buy-in at every level, and following up over time to make sure the change actually holds after implementation ends.

Making Change Last

The best systems are the ones a team will actually use. That means making the purpose clear, reinforcing early wins, and building genuine ownership in how the work gets done.

No endless meetings. No complicated reports. Just practical improvements that keep the organization running long after the engagement ends.

I started Fieldwise because I kept seeing the same pattern. As businesses add locations, crews, or customers, they naturally outgrow the informal systems that got them there. Stronger systems aren't a sign something's broken, they're what keeps a growing business running well.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need new software?

Probably not. Most businesses already have more software than they need. The issue is usually execution, consistency, communication, or visibility. I'll first determine how to make what you already have work better before recommending anything new.

Will you implement AI?

Only if it genuinely makes your business easier to operate. AI isn't the starting point. We first focus on creating lasting behavioral change, accountability, and consistency in how things get done. Then we'll determine where technology can genuinely save time.

What types of businesses do you work with?

Established trade and service businesses, typically $1M+ in annual revenue, where growth is accelerating and complexity is increasing. That often looks like communication becoming more difficult, consistency getting harder to maintain across crews or locations, or leadership wanting greater visibility before small issues become expensive ones.

How quickly will I see improvements?

Many owners leave the very first conversation seeing their business differently. Some improvements happen immediately. The biggest improvements typically happen during the first 90 days, because implementation begins right away.

How much money can this save my business?

A disorganized business is a drain on how much more money you could be taking home each year. Miscommunication, rework, repetitive tasks, fragmented systems, and misaligned processes all add up. The goal is to continue building improvements that earn you more, year after year.
